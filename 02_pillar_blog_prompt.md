# prompts/02_pillar_blog_prompt.md

> **Reusable Prompt: 2000+ Word SEO Pillar Blog Generator**
> Use this prompt to generate a long-form, authoritative blog that targets your primary keyword and builds topical authority for the entire content cluster.

---

## What is a Pillar Blog?

A pillar blog is the main, comprehensive article around which all supporting blogs (the content cluster) are organized. It:

- Targets the **primary keyword**
- Covers the topic **in depth** (2000+ words)
- Links **outward** to supporting blogs
- Earns **backlinks** because it is genuinely useful
- Signals **topical authority** to Google

---

## Prompt Template

```
You are a professional SEO content writer and educational marketing specialist.

Write a complete, SEO-optimized pillar blog article for the following business:

Business Name: [BUSINESS_NAME]
Location: [CITY], [STATE]
Business Type: [BUSINESS_TYPE]
Primary Keyword: [PRIMARY_KEYWORD]
Target Audience: [TARGET_AUDIENCE]

---

### BLOG REQUIREMENTS

**Length:** 2000–2500 words (not counting the meta fields)

**Tone:** Helpful, clear, and encouraging. Avoid jargon. Write for someone who is genuinely searching for this service and wants honest guidance.

**Structure — generate each section in order:**

---

**SEO TITLE**
Under 60 characters. Must include [PRIMARY_KEYWORD]. Add a compelling hook.

**META DESCRIPTION**
140–155 characters. Include [PRIMARY_KEYWORD] and a clear call to action.

**SLUG**
URL-friendly. Lowercase. Hyphens only. Example: best-spoken-english-classes-t-nagar-chennai

---

**H1 (Main Heading)**
Must include the primary keyword naturally. Should feel like a clear, honest promise to the reader.

---

**Introduction (150–200 words)**
- Open with a relatable problem or aspiration the reader has
- Acknowledge why this topic matters
- State clearly what this blog will help them find
- Do NOT make unverifiable claims about rankings, awards, or placements

---

**H2: Why Spoken English Skills Matter in Today's World**
Explain the real-world demand. Connect it to the reader's career or life goals. (~200 words)

---

**H2: What to Look for in a Spoken English Institute**
List 5–6 genuine factors a student should consider when choosing a class. Include:
- Teaching methodology
- Batch sizes
- Practice opportunities
- Trainer qualifications
- Course duration and fees transparency
(~300 words)

---

**H2: Course Structure — What a Good Program Looks Like**
Describe a realistic, well-structured course curriculum. Include modules such as:
- Foundation English
- Pronunciation and accent
- Grammar in conversation
- Listening and comprehension
- Public speaking
- Interview and professional English
(~300 words)

---

**H2: Who Can Benefit From Spoken English Classes?**
Write short paragraphs for:
- Students
- Job seekers
- Working professionals
- Business owners
- Homemakers returning to work
(~250 words)

---

**H2: Tips to Make the Most of Your English Course**
Give 5–6 practical, actionable tips a student can apply both inside and outside the classroom. (~200 words)

---

**H2: What to Expect in Your First Week**
Set realistic expectations. Help the reader visualize starting the course. (~150 words)

---

**H2: Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)**
Write 5 FAQs based on real questions people ask about spoken English classes.
For each question, write a helpful 2–3 sentence answer.

Suggested questions:
- How long does it take to become fluent in spoken English?
- Can I join if my grammar is very weak?
- Are online spoken English classes effective?
- What is the difference between spoken English and written English?
- How do I choose between multiple institutes in [CITY]?

---

**H2: Conclusion**
Summarize the key points. Encourage the reader to take action. (~150 words)

---

**CTA (Call to Action)**
Write a clear, friendly CTA encouraging the reader to:
- Visit the institute
- Call or WhatsApp for a free demo class
- Ask any questions before enrolling
Do NOT use pushy sales language.

---

**Internal Link Suggestions**
List 4–5 anchor texts and target blog titles for internal links within this article.

**External Reference Suggestions**
Suggest 2–3 types of authoritative external sources to cite (e.g., government employment reports, academic language studies) — do not invent URLs.

---

Rules:
- Write in full paragraphs, not bullet dumps
- No keyword stuffing — use the primary keyword naturally 6–8 times
- Every H2 section must have at least 2–3 paragraphs
- Do not invent fake reviews, awards, placements, or statistics
- Keep the language accessible (Grade 8–10 reading level)
```

## Customization Tips

- To target **job seekers specifically**, add: "Emphasize career outcomes and interview preparation throughout."
- To target **students**, add: "Include a section on how this helps with campus placement drives."
- To write for a **Hindi-speaking audience**, add: "Note that the institute offers support for Hindi-speaking learners adjusting to English communication."
